# Commercial Lease Review Checklist
**Kim Weatherington | Legal Operations Portfolio**

---

## Purpose
This checklist is used during initial and secondary review of commercial lease agreements to identify risk provisions, flag missing terms, and ensure all critical business and legal interests are protected prior to execution.

---

## 1. Parties & Premises

- [ ] Full legal names of Landlord and Tenant confirmed
- [ ] Entity type and state of formation verified (LLC, Corp, etc.)
- [ ] Authorized signatory identified and confirmed
- [ ] Premises address and suite/unit number accurately described
- [ ] Square footage defined and method of measurement specified (RSF vs. USF)
- [ ] Common areas and exclusions clearly identified

---

## 2. Lease Term & Options

- [ ] Commencement date clearly defined (fixed or condition-based)
- [ ] Expiration date confirmed
- [ ] Renewal/extension options: number of options, notice period, rent terms
- [ ] Early termination rights and associated penalties
- [ ] Holdover provisions reviewed — month-to-month rate confirmed
- [ ] Right of first refusal or right of first offer on adjacent space

---

## 3. Rent & Financial Terms

- [ ] Base rent amount, frequency, and due date confirmed
- [ ] Rent escalation/step-up schedule reviewed (fixed % or CPI-based)
- [ ] Free rent / rent abatement periods clearly stated
- [ ] Security deposit amount, terms, and conditions for return
- [ ] Late fees and grace period defined
- [ ] Triple net (NNN) vs. gross lease structure confirmed
- [ ] Operating expense inclusions/exclusions reviewed
- [ ] CAM charges capped or uncapped — cap percentage confirmed
- [ ] Audit rights for operating expenses included

---

## 4. Use & Exclusivity

- [ ] Permitted use clause: specific enough to protect operations, broad enough for flexibility
- [ ] Exclusive use provision negotiated (if applicable)
- [ ] Prohibited use restrictions reviewed — no conflicts with business operations
- [ ] Operating covenant (required hours of operation) reviewed

---

## 5. Improvements & Buildout

- [ ] Tenant improvement allowance (TIA) amount and disbursement terms
- [ ] Landlord's work vs. Tenant's work clearly delineated
- [ ] Approval process for alterations and improvements
- [ ] Restoration obligations at lease end — what must be removed
- [ ] Ownership of improvements at expiration

---

## 6. Assignment & Subletting

- [ ] Assignment rights and Landlord consent requirements
- [ ] Subletting rights and notice requirements
- [ ] Recapture rights — Landlord's ability to reclaim space
- [ ] Permitted transfers (affiliates, mergers, acquisitions) defined

---

## 7. Default & Remedies

- [ ] Tenant default triggers (monetary and non-monetary)
- [ ] Cure periods for each type of default
- [ ] Landlord default provisions and Tenant's remedies
- [ ] Self-help rights for Tenant (if any)
- [ ] Landlord's right to terminate vs. right to recover damages
- [ ] Personal guarantee required — scope and duration reviewed

---

## 8. Insurance & Indemnification

- [ ] Required insurance types and minimum coverage amounts
- [ ] Additional insured requirements
- [ ] Waiver of subrogation provisions
- [ ] Indemnification obligations — mutual or one-sided
- [ ] Landlord's insurance obligations

---

## 9. Maintenance & Repairs

- [ ] Landlord maintenance responsibilities (roof, structure, systems)
- [ ] Tenant maintenance responsibilities (interior, HVAC, etc.)
- [ ] HVAC maintenance obligations — who services, who replaces
- [ ] Compliance with laws: ADA, building codes, environmental

---

## 10. Risk & Compliance Flags 🚩

| Issue | Notes | Status |
|---|---|---|
| Rent escalation exceeds market rate | | |
| No CAP on operating expense increases | | |
| Exclusivity provision absent or too narrow | | |
| Assignment requires Landlord consent with no standard | | |
| Personal guarantee unlimited in duration | | |
| Restoration obligations are broad / costly | | |
| No Tenant default cure period stated | | |
| Insurance requirements disproportionate | | |
